---

# Purpose

The purpose of this Internet Usage Policy is to ensure that employees use the organization's internet resources safely, responsibly, and securely. Proper internet usage reduces cybersecurity risks such as malware infections, phishing attacks, ransomware, data breaches, and unauthorized access.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- Company-owned computers
- Laptops
- Mobile devices
- Office Wi-Fi
- Corporate VPN
- Cloud services
- Remote employees
- Any device connected to the company network

---

# Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

- Promote safe internet browsing.
- Protect company information.
- Prevent malware infections.
- Reduce cybersecurity risks.
- Maintain employee productivity.
- Ensure compliance with organizational policies.

---

# Acceptable Internet Usage

Employees may use the internet for:

- Business research
- Customer support
- Vendor communication
- Online training
- Software updates
- Cloud-based business applications
- Government and regulatory websites
- Professional development

Limited personal browsing is allowed if it:

- Does not interfere with work.
- Does not consume excessive bandwidth.
- Does not violate company policies.
- Does not expose the organization to security risks.

---

# Prohibited Internet Activities

Employees must not:

- Visit illegal websites.
- Download pirated software.
- Access gambling websites.
- Visit adult or inappropriate websites.
- Download copyrighted material without authorization.
- Participate in illegal online activities.
- Install browser extensions without approval.
- Use anonymous proxy services or unauthorized VPNs.
- Circumvent company security controls.

---

# Safe Browsing Practices

Employees should:

- Verify website addresses before entering credentials.
- Ensure websites use HTTPS whenever possible.
- Avoid clicking suspicious advertisements.
- Close unexpected pop-up windows.
- Download files only from trusted sources.
- Keep browsers updated.

---

# Download Policy

Employees may download:

- Approved business software
- Company documents
- Vendor resources
- Work-related files

Employees must not download:

- Unauthorized software
- Cracked applications
- Pirated media
- Unknown executable files
- Suspicious email attachments

All downloaded files should be scanned with antivirus software before opening.

---

# Social Media Usage

Employees may access social media for:

- Official company marketing
- Customer engagement
- Business networking
- Industry research

Employees must not:

- Share confidential company information.
- Post offensive or discriminatory content using company accounts.
- Represent the organization without authorization.
- Discuss sensitive business matters publicly.

---

# Cloud Storage Usage

Approved cloud services may be used for:

- Business documents
- Project collaboration
- Backup storage

Employees must not:

- Upload confidential data to personal cloud accounts.
- Share confidential files through unauthorized services.
- Disable security settings on cloud platforms.

---

# Streaming and Multimedia

Employees should avoid excessive use of:

- Video streaming
- Music streaming
- Online gaming
- Large personal downloads

These activities may consume excessive network bandwidth and affect business operations.

---

# Remote Internet Access

Employees working remotely must:

- Use the company VPN.
- Connect through secure Wi-Fi.
- Avoid public Wi-Fi whenever possible.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Keep devices updated.

---

# Malware Protection

Employees must:

- Keep antivirus software enabled.
- Allow automatic security updates.
- Report suspicious downloads.
- Never disable endpoint protection.

---

# Web Filtering

The organization may block access to:

- Malicious websites
- Phishing websites
- Gambling websites
- Adult content
- Known malware domains
- Unauthorized file-sharing services

---

# Monitoring

The organization reserves the right to monitor:

- Internet browsing activity
- Download history
- Bandwidth usage
- Security alerts
- Access to restricted websites
- Web traffic logs

Monitoring is conducted for security, operational, and compliance purposes.

---

# Incident Reporting

Employees must immediately report:

- Malicious websites
- Suspicious downloads
- Browser warnings
- Malware infections
- Phishing attempts
- Unauthorized internet activity

Reports should be submitted to the IT Administrator.

---

# Employee Responsibilities

Employees must:

- Follow this policy.
- Use the internet responsibly.
- Protect company information.
- Report suspicious activity.
- Complete cybersecurity awareness training.

---

# IT Administrator Responsibilities

The IT Administrator shall:

- Monitor internet activity.
- Configure web filtering.
- Maintain firewalls.
- Update security software.
- Investigate internet-related incidents.
- Block malicious websites.

---

# Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Restricted internet access
- Disciplinary action
- Suspension of user privileges
- Internal investigation
- Legal action where applicable

---

# Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed:

- Every 12 months
- After significant cybersecurity incidents
- Following infrastructure changes
- When legal or regulatory requirements change
