# Purpose

The purpose of this Physical Security Policy is to protect the organization's personnel, facilities, equipment, and information assets from unauthorized physical access, theft, vandalism, and environmental hazards. Effective physical security complements cybersecurity by preventing physical compromise of systems and sensitive information.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- Office buildings
- Server rooms
- Data storage areas
- Workstations
- Laptops and mobile devices
- Network equipment
- Visitors
- Third-party vendors
- Company-owned assets

---

# Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

- Prevent unauthorized physical access.
- Protect critical IT infrastructure.
- Safeguard confidential information.
- Reduce theft and vandalism.
- Support business continuity.
- Ensure employee safety.

---

# Physical Access Control

Access to company facilities shall be restricted using approved security measures such as:

- Employee ID cards
- Access badges
- Electronic access control systems
- Security guards (where applicable)
- Visitor management procedures

Only authorized personnel may enter restricted areas.

---

# Restricted Areas

The following locations are considered restricted:

- Server room
- Network equipment room
- Backup storage room
- Finance department
- HR records room
- Security operations area

Access must be granted only to authorized personnel with a valid business need.

---

# Visitor Management

All visitors must:

- Sign the visitor register upon arrival.
- Present valid identification if requested.
- Wear a visitor badge while on-site.
- Be escorted in restricted areas.
- Return visitor badges before leaving.

Visitors must not access company systems or confidential information without authorization.

---

# Employee Identification

Employees must:

- Wear company identification badges when required.
- Keep identification cards secure.
- Report lost or stolen badges immediately.
- Never lend identification cards to others.

---

# Server Room Security

The server room must:

- Remain locked at all times.
- Be accessible only to authorized IT personnel.
- Maintain an access log.
- Be protected by environmental controls.
- Contain fire detection and suppression systems where feasible.

No food or drinks are permitted in the server room.

---

# Equipment Security

Employees must:

- Lock computers when unattended.
- Secure laptops with cable locks where appropriate.
- Store portable devices safely.
- Keep sensitive equipment away from public areas.
- Report damaged or missing equipment immediately.

---

# Clean Desk Policy

At the end of each workday, employees should:

- Lock confidential documents in secure cabinets.
- Remove sensitive information from desks.
- Log out of company systems.
- Lock computers.
- Secure portable devices.

---

# Media Protection

Physical storage media such as USB drives, external hard drives, and backup tapes must:

- Be encrypted where applicable.
- Be stored in secure locations.
- Be accessible only to authorized personnel.
- Be securely destroyed when no longer needed.

---

# Environmental Controls

Critical areas should be protected against environmental threats by implementing:

- Fire alarms
- Smoke detectors
- Fire extinguishers
- Temperature monitoring
- Humidity control
- Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS)
- Surge protection

---

# CCTV Monitoring

Where installed, CCTV systems may be used to monitor:

- Building entrances
- Exits
- Hallways
- Server rooms
- Parking areas
- Storage rooms

Video recordings should be retained according to company policy and applicable legal requirements.

---

# Asset Management

The organization shall maintain an inventory of company assets, including:

- Computers
- Laptops
- Mobile devices
- Servers
- Network equipment
- Storage devices

Asset records should include:

- Asset ID
- Serial number
- Assigned user
- Location
- Purchase date
- Current status

---

# Device Disposal

Before disposal or recycling, storage devices must be securely sanitized using approved methods such as:

- Secure data wiping
- Physical destruction
- Certified disposal services

Confidential information must not remain recoverable.

---

# Emergency Procedures

Employees must know how to respond to:

- Fire emergencies
- Power outages
- Flooding
- Earthquakes
- Medical emergencies
- Security threats

Emergency exits and evacuation routes should remain clearly marked and unobstructed.

---

# Incident Reporting

Employees must immediately report:

- Unauthorized access
- Lost or stolen equipment
- Damaged security controls
- Suspicious individuals
- Physical theft
- Facility damage

Reports should be submitted to management or the IT Administrator without delay.

---

# Responsibilities

## Employees

Employees are responsible for:

- Protecting company assets.
- Following physical security procedures.
- Reporting security incidents.
- Wearing identification when required.
- Keeping work areas secure.

---

## IT Administrator

The IT Administrator is responsible for:

- Protecting IT equipment.
- Managing server room access.
- Maintaining asset inventories.
- Supporting secure device disposal.
- Investigating physical security incidents involving technology.

---

## Management

Management is responsible for:

- Approving physical security measures.
- Providing security awareness training.
- Reviewing physical security risks.
- Ensuring compliance with this policy.

---

# Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Loss of facility access.
- Disciplinary action.
- Security investigation.
- Financial liability for negligence.
- Legal action where applicable.

---

# Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed:

- Every 12 months.
- After physical security incidents.
- Following facility renovations.
- When business operations change.

# Conclusion

Physical security is a critical component of an organization's overall security strategy. By implementing strong access controls, protecting facilities and equipment, managing visitors, and responding effectively to physical threats, the organization can reduce risks and support the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of its information assets.
