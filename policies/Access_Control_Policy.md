---

# Purpose

The purpose of this Access Control Policy is to ensure that only authorized individuals have access to the organization's information systems, applications, networks, and data. Access rights are granted based on business requirements and the Principle of Least Privilege.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- Employees
- Contractors
- Temporary staff
- Third-party vendors
- Company-owned devices
- Cloud services
- Internal networks
- Business applications
- Servers and databases

---

# Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

- Prevent unauthorized access.
- Protect sensitive business information.
- Ensure accountability for system usage.
- Reduce insider threats.
- Support regulatory compliance.
- Maintain confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.

---

# Access Control Principles

The organization follows these security principles:

- Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Need-to-Know Principle
- Separation of Duties
- Zero Trust Security Model

---

# User Account Management

Each employee shall receive:

- One unique user account.
- Appropriate access based on job responsibilities.
- Secure authentication credentials.

Shared accounts are prohibited unless specifically approved by management.

---

# Account Creation

New user accounts require:

- Approval from the employee's manager.
- Approval from the IT Administrator.
- Assignment of appropriate permissions.
- Acceptance of company security policies.

---

# Account Modification

User accounts must be updated whenever:

- Job roles change.
- Departments change.
- Additional responsibilities are assigned.
- Access requirements change.

---

# Account Deactivation

Accounts must be disabled immediately when:

- Employment ends.
- A contract expires.
- An employee resigns.
- Unauthorized activity is detected.

Inactive accounts older than **90 days** should be reviewed and removed.

---

# Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Access is assigned according to job responsibilities.

| Role | Access Level |
|------|--------------|
| Business Owner | Full Access |
| IT Administrator | Administrative Access |
| HR Staff | HR Systems Only |
| Finance Staff | Financial Systems |
| Sales Team | CRM and Sales Applications |
| Customer Support | Customer Support Portal |
| General Employees | Standard User Access |

---

# Privileged Accounts

Administrator accounts must:

- Be used only for administrative tasks.
- Have Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) enabled.
- Use strong passwords.
- Be monitored regularly.
- Never be shared.

---

# Remote Access

Employees accessing company systems remotely must:

- Use an approved VPN.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
- Use company-approved devices whenever possible.
- Connect only through secure networks.
- Keep operating systems updated.

---

# Physical Access

Access to physical facilities must be restricted.

Security controls include:

- Employee ID cards
- Locked server rooms
- CCTV monitoring
- Visitor registration
- Access logs

Only authorized personnel may enter restricted areas.

---

# Authentication Requirements

Authentication methods include:

- Strong passwords
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Biometric authentication (where available)
- Security keys for privileged users

---

# Authorization

Access permissions are granted based on:

- Business requirements
- Job responsibilities
- Management approval
- Security policies

Users must not access systems outside their assigned responsibilities.

---

# Access Reviews

Access permissions shall be reviewed:

- Every six months.
- After employee role changes.
- After organizational restructuring.
- Following security incidents.

Unused or unnecessary permissions must be removed promptly.

---

# Third-Party Access

Third-party vendors and contractors:

- Must sign confidentiality agreements.
- Receive only the minimum required access.
- Use unique user accounts.
- Have accounts disabled after contract completion.

---

# Monitoring and Logging

The IT department shall monitor:

- Login attempts
- Failed authentication attempts
- Privileged account activity
- Remote access sessions
- Account modifications
- Access to sensitive systems

Logs should be retained according to organizational policies.

---

# Prohibited Activities

The following activities are prohibited:

- Sharing user accounts.
- Using another person's credentials.
- Attempting unauthorized access.
- Bypassing authentication controls.
- Creating unauthorized accounts.
- Escalating privileges without approval.

---

# User Responsibilities

All users must:

- Protect login credentials.
- Lock computers when unattended.
- Report suspicious account activity.
- Follow company cybersecurity policies.
- Log out after completing work.

---

# IT Administrator Responsibilities

The IT Administrator shall:

- Create and manage user accounts.
- Approve access requests.
- Conduct periodic access reviews.
- Monitor authentication logs.
- Disable inactive accounts.
- Investigate unauthorized access attempts.

---

# Incident Reporting

Employees must immediately report:

- Unauthorized access attempts
- Lost credentials
- Suspicious login notifications
- Privilege misuse
- Account compromise

Reports should be submitted to the IT Administrator without delay.

---

# Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Suspension of user accounts
- Disciplinary action
- Loss of access privileges
- Internal investigation
- Legal action where applicable

---

# Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed:

- Annually
- After major cybersecurity incidents
- Following infrastructure changes
- When regulatory requirements change

# Conclusion

Effective access control is essential for protecting organizational resources from unauthorized access and misuse. By implementing role-based access, enforcing strong authentication, regularly reviewing permissions, and monitoring user activity, the organization can significantly reduce security risks and maintain a secure computing environment.
