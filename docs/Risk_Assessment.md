# Risk Assessment

## Overview

Risk assessment is the process of identifying, analyzing, and evaluating potential cybersecurity risks that could negatively impact an organization's information systems, business operations, and sensitive data. This assessment helps prioritize security controls based on the likelihood and impact of potential threats.

---

# Purpose

The purpose of this risk assessment is to:

- Identify valuable business assets.
- Identify possible cybersecurity threats.
- Discover system vulnerabilities.
- Evaluate the level of risk.
- Recommend appropriate security controls.
- Reduce the likelihood of successful cyberattacks.

# Recommended Security Controls

To improve cybersecurity, the following controls should be implemented:

## Administrative Controls

- Security Awareness Training
- Password Policy
- Acceptable Use Policy
- Incident Response Plan
- Access Control Policy
- Data Classification Policy

---

## Technical Controls

- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)
- Email Spam Filtering
- Encryption for Sensitive Data
- VPN for Remote Employees
- Network Firewall
- Intrusion Detection System (IDS)
- Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)

---

## Physical Controls

- CCTV Cameras
- Visitor Log Register
- Locked Server Room
- Employee ID Cards
- Secure Equipment Storage

---

# Risk Treatment Plan

| Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|---------------------|
| Phishing | Employee awareness training and email filtering |
| Malware | Antivirus and EDR solutions |
| Weak Passwords | Strong password policy and MFA |
| Data Breach | Encryption and access control |
| Ransomware | Offline backups and endpoint protection |
| Insider Threat | Least privilege access and monitoring |
| Hardware Failure | Redundant systems and regular backups |

---

# Residual Risk

Even after implementing security controls, some risks remain. These risks should be continuously monitored and reviewed through periodic security assessments, vulnerability scans, penetration testing, and employee awareness programs.

---

# Review Schedule

Risk assessments should be performed:

- Every 12 months
- After major security incidents
- After significant infrastructure changes
- After introducing new software or hardware
- Following regulatory updates

---

# Conclusion

This risk assessment identifies the primary cybersecurity threats and vulnerabilities affecting a small business. By implementing the recommended administrative, technical, and physical security controls, the organization can significantly reduce cyber risks, protect sensitive information, and improve its overall security posture. Continuous monitoring and periodic risk assessments are essential to maintain an effective cybersecurity program.
