---

# Purpose

Email is one of the primary communication methods within the organization and is also one of the most common attack vectors used by cybercriminals. This policy establishes guidelines for the secure use of email to protect confidential information, reduce phishing risks, and maintain business continuity.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- Company email accounts
- Desktop email applications
- Webmail services
- Mobile email applications
- Cloud-based email platforms
- Third-party email services used for business

---

# Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

- Protect business communications.
- Prevent phishing attacks.
- Reduce malware infections.
- Secure confidential information.
- Ensure compliance with company security policies.
- Promote responsible email usage.

---

# Acceptable Email Usage

Employees may use company email for:

- Business communication
- Customer support
- Internal collaboration
- Vendor communication
- Project coordination
- Official company announcements

Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work responsibilities or violate company policies.

---

# Prohibited Activities

Employees must not:

- Send confidential information without authorization.
- Open suspicious attachments.
- Click unknown links.
- Forward chain emails.
- Send offensive or inappropriate content.
- Use company email for illegal activities.
- Share company credentials through email.
- Bypass email security controls.

---

# Phishing Protection

Employees should:

- Verify the sender's email address.
- Check for spelling and grammar mistakes.
- Be cautious of urgent requests.
- Confirm payment requests through another communication channel.
- Report suspicious emails immediately.

Never provide:

- Passwords
- One-Time Passwords (OTPs)
- Banking information
- Customer data
- Personal identification information

---

# Business Email Compromise (BEC)

To prevent BEC attacks:

- Verify payment requests.
- Confirm invoice changes by phone.
- Verify changes to bank account details.
- Require management approval for large financial transactions.

---

# Email Attachments

Employees should:

- Open attachments only from trusted sources.
- Scan downloaded files with antivirus software.
- Avoid opening executable files received through email.
- Report unexpected attachments to the IT department.

The following file types require additional verification:

- .exe
- .bat
- .cmd
- .js
- .vbs
- .scr
- .zip
- .rar

---

# Email Links

Before clicking a link:

- Hover over the link to verify the destination.
- Check the domain name carefully.
- Ensure HTTPS is used where applicable.
- Do not click shortened or suspicious URLs unless verified.

---

# Email Encryption

Sensitive information transmitted through email should:

- Be encrypted whenever possible.
- Use approved encryption methods.
- Be shared only with authorized recipients.

Examples of sensitive information include:

- Customer records
- Financial reports
- Employee information
- Contracts
- Business plans

---

# Spam Protection

The organization shall:

- Use spam filtering solutions.
- Block malicious senders.
- Update spam rules regularly.
- Quarantine suspicious emails.

Employees should avoid interacting with spam messages.

---

# Email Retention

Business emails should be retained according to organizational requirements.

Recommended retention periods:

| Email Type | Retention Period |
|------------|------------------|
| General Business | 2 Years |
| Financial Records | 7 Years |
| Legal Documents | As Required |
| HR Records | As Required |

---

# Mobile Email Security

Employees accessing email on mobile devices must:

- Use screen locks.
- Enable device encryption.
- Keep devices updated.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Report lost devices immediately.

---

# Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA is mandatory for:

- Company email accounts
- Administrator accounts
- Remote email access
- Cloud-based email services

---

# Monitoring

The organization reserves the right to monitor:

- Email traffic
- Spam activity
- Malware detections
- Suspicious login attempts
- Unauthorized forwarding rules

Monitoring is conducted to maintain security and compliance.

---

# Incident Reporting

Employees must immediately report:

- Phishing emails
- Suspicious attachments
- Accidental disclosure of confidential information
- Unauthorized access to email accounts
- Suspected malware infections

Reports should be submitted to the IT Administrator as soon as possible.

---

# Employee Responsibilities

Employees must:

- Follow this policy.
- Protect email credentials.
- Verify unknown senders.
- Report suspicious emails.
- Use company email responsibly.

---

# IT Administrator Responsibilities

The IT Administrator shall:

- Configure spam filters.
- Enable email security features.
- Monitor email threats.
- Manage email accounts.
- Investigate email-related incidents.
- Provide employee awareness training.

---

# Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Suspension of email access
- Disciplinary action
- Security investigation
- Legal action where applicable

---

# Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed:

- Every 12 months
- After major email security incidents
- Following changes to email infrastructure
- When regulatory requirements change

# Conclusion

Email remains one of the most targeted communication channels for cyberattacks. By following this Email Security Policy, employees can help protect the organization from phishing, malware, business email compromise, and data leakage while ensuring secure and reliable business communication.
