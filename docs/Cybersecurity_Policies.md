# Cybersecurity Policies

## Overview

Cybersecurity policies establish the rules, standards, and procedures that employees and contractors must follow to protect the organization's information systems, networks, and data. These policies help reduce cyber risks, ensure regulatory compliance, and promote a strong security culture.

---

# Policy Objectives

The objectives of these policies are to:

- Protect confidential business information.
- Prevent unauthorized access to systems.
- Reduce cybersecurity risks.
- Ensure compliance with industry standards.
- Promote responsible use of company resources.
- Improve employee awareness of cybersecurity threats.

---

# 1. Acceptable Use Policy

## Purpose

Ensure that company IT resources are used responsibly.

### Policy

Employees must:

- Use company devices only for authorized business purposes.
- Follow all company security procedures.
- Report suspicious activities immediately.
- Keep work-related information confidential.

Employees must not:

- Install unauthorized software.
- Visit malicious or illegal websites.
- Share company credentials.
- Download pirated software.
- Disable security software.

---

# 2. Password Policy

## Purpose

Protect user accounts against unauthorized access.

### Requirements

- Minimum password length: **12 characters**
- Use uppercase and lowercase letters.
- Include numbers.
- Include special characters.
- Do not reuse previous passwords.
- Change passwords every **90 days**.
- Never share passwords.
- Use a password manager whenever possible.

### Example

**Strong Password**

```
Tech@2026!Secure
```

---

# 3. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Policy

## Purpose

Provide an additional layer of security.

### Requirements

MFA must be enabled for:

- Email accounts
- VPN access
- Cloud services
- Administrator accounts
- Financial systems

Approved methods include:

- Authenticator applications
- Security keys
- Biometric authentication

---

# 4. Access Control Policy

## Purpose

Limit system access to authorized users.

### Rules

- Every employee receives an individual account.
- Shared accounts are prohibited.
- Access is granted based on job responsibilities.
- Administrator privileges are limited.
- Remove access immediately when an employee leaves the company.

---

# 5. Email Security Policy

## Purpose

Reduce phishing and email-based attacks.

### Employees should:

- Verify unknown senders.
- Avoid opening suspicious attachments.
- Never click unknown links.
- Report phishing emails to IT.
- Use company email only for business communication.

---

# 6. Internet Usage Policy

## Purpose

Ensure safe internet browsing.

### Employees must not:

- Download illegal content.
- Visit malicious websites.
- Access gambling or adult websites.
- Download unauthorized software.
- Bypass company security controls.

---

# 7. Data Protection Policy

## Purpose

Protect sensitive business information.

### Data Classification

| Classification | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Public | Information available to everyone |
| Internal | Internal business information |
| Confidential | Sensitive company information |
| Restricted | Highly sensitive business data |

### Rules

- Encrypt confidential data.
- Limit access to sensitive information.
- Securely dispose of confidential documents.
- Store backups securely.

---

# 8. Backup Policy

## Purpose

Ensure business data can be recovered.

### Backup Schedule

| Type | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| Critical Data | Daily |
| Full Backup | Weekly |
| Archive Backup | Monthly |

### Requirements

- Test backups regularly.
- Keep one offline backup.
- Store cloud backups securely.
- Encrypt backup files.

---

# 9. Remote Work Policy

## Purpose

Protect company resources outside the office.

### Employees must:

- Use VPN connections.
- Enable MFA.
- Lock computers when unattended.
- Use secure home Wi-Fi.
- Keep operating systems updated.

---

# 10. Mobile Device Policy

Employees using smartphones or tablets for work must:

- Enable screen locks.
- Encrypt the device.
- Keep software updated.
- Report lost devices immediately.
- Install approved security software.

---

# 11. Physical Security Policy

### Requirements

- Lock server rooms.
- Use employee ID cards.
- Restrict visitor access.
- Install CCTV cameras.
- Secure networking equipment.

---

# 12. Software Update Policy

### Requirements

- Install security patches promptly.
- Enable automatic updates.
- Remove unsupported software.
- Update antivirus definitions daily.

---

# 13. Incident Reporting Policy

Employees must immediately report:

- Phishing emails
- Malware infections
- Lost devices
- Data leaks
- Unauthorized access
- Suspicious activities

Reports should include:

- Date and time
- System affected
- Description of the incident
- Screenshots (if available)

---

# 14. Security Awareness Policy

Employees shall complete cybersecurity awareness training:

- During onboarding
- Every six months
- After major security incidents

Training topics include:

- Phishing
- Password security
- Social engineering
- Safe browsing
- Data protection
- Incident reporting

---

# 15. Compliance Policy

The organization follows industry best practices, including:

- ISO/IEC 27001
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
- CIS Critical Security Controls
- OWASP Top 10

Regular audits and reviews will ensure continued compliance.

---

# Policy Review

These cybersecurity policies shall be reviewed:

- Every 12 months
- After major cybersecurity incidents
- Following significant infrastructure changes
- When new regulatory requirements are introduced

---

# Responsibilities

## Management

- Approve cybersecurity policies.
- Allocate security resources.
- Promote a security-first culture.

## IT Department

- Implement technical controls.
- Monitor systems.
- Respond to security incidents.
- Maintain backups.

## Employees

- Follow all cybersecurity policies.
- Protect company credentials.
- Report suspicious activities.
- Complete required security training.

---

# Conclusion

These cybersecurity policies provide a comprehensive framework for protecting a small business against modern cyber threats. By following these guidelines, the organization can improve its security posture, reduce operational risks, safeguard sensitive information, and support business continuity through consistent and secure practices.
