# Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Policy

## Document Information

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Policy Name | Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Policy |
| Version | 1.0 |
| Effective Date | July 2026 |
| Review Frequency | Annual |
| Applies To | All Employees, Contractors, Interns, and Consultants |

---

# Purpose

The purpose of this Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Policy is to establish security requirements for employees who use personally owned devices to access company systems, applications, networks, or data.

This policy helps protect organizational information while allowing employees to work efficiently using approved personal devices.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- Personal laptops
- Personal desktop computers
- Smartphones
- Tablets
- Smart wearable devices (when approved)
- Personally owned storage devices
- Any personal device used to access company resources

---

# Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

- Protect company data on personal devices.
- Reduce cybersecurity risks.
- Prevent unauthorized access.
- Protect customer information.
- Ensure secure remote work.
- Maintain compliance with organizational security policies.

---

# Eligibility

Employees may use personal devices only after:

- Management approval.
- IT department approval.
- Device security verification.
- Acceptance of this policy.

Unauthorized devices are not permitted to access company resources.

---

# Approved Devices

The following devices may be approved:

- Windows laptops
- macOS laptops
- Linux laptops
- Android smartphones
- iPhones
- Tablets

Devices that no longer receive security updates are not permitted.

---

# Device Registration

Before accessing company systems, employees must register their device with the IT department.

Registration includes:

- Device owner
- Device type
- Operating system
- Device serial number
- MAC address (if required)

---

# Security Requirements

All BYOD devices must:

- Use strong passwords.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Have screen lock enabled.
- Enable full-disk encryption.
- Install approved antivirus software.
- Enable automatic updates.
- Keep the operating system fully updated.

---

# Password Requirements

Passwords must:

- Be at least 12 characters long.
- Contain uppercase and lowercase letters.
- Include numbers.
- Include special characters.
- Never be shared.
- Be changed immediately if compromise is suspected.

---

# Device Encryption

The following must be encrypted:

- Local storage
- Removable storage devices
- Business documents
- Cached company emails

Encryption helps protect data if the device is lost or stolen.

---

# Company Data

Employees must:

- Store company data only in approved locations.
- Avoid copying confidential information to personal folders.
- Use company-approved cloud storage.
- Delete company information when instructed.

Company data remains the property of the organization.

---

# Network Access

Personal devices accessing company resources must:

- Connect using the company VPN.
- Use secure Wi-Fi.
- Avoid public Wi-Fi whenever possible.
- Enable firewall protection.

---

# Software Requirements

Employees must not:

- Install pirated software.
- Disable antivirus software.
- Disable operating system security features.
- Install unauthorized applications that access company information.

Only licensed and trusted software should be used.

---

# Lost or Stolen Devices

If a personal device containing company information is lost or stolen:

1. Notify the IT department immediately.
2. Change company passwords.
3. Allow remote removal of company data if supported.
4. Cooperate with the security investigation.

Reports should be made within one hour of discovery whenever possible.

---

# Remote Wipe

Where technically possible and previously agreed upon, the organization may remove **company-owned data only** from registered BYOD devices when:

- The device is lost or stolen.
- Employment ends.
- Access authorization is revoked.
- A serious security incident occurs.

The organization will aim to avoid deleting personal files unless required to protect company information.

---

# Prohibited Activities

Employees must not:

- Share company information with unauthorized persons.
- Root or jailbreak devices used for work.
- Install malicious software.
- Disable device encryption.
- Bypass security controls.
- Share company credentials.

---

# Monitoring

The organization may monitor:

- Device compliance status
- Security software status
- Company application usage
- Authentication logs
- Access to company resources

The organization does **not** monitor personal content such as personal photos, personal messages, or personal emails, except where required by law or during an authorized security investigation.

---

# Employee Responsibilities

Employees must:

- Protect company information.
- Keep devices updated.
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately.
- Follow all cybersecurity policies.
- Allow security inspections when required for company data protection.

---

# IT Administrator Responsibilities

The IT Administrator shall:

- Register approved devices.
- Configure secure access.
- Verify device compliance.
- Provide technical support.
- Respond to security incidents.
- Remove company access when required.

---

# Offboarding

When an employee leaves the organization:

- Company accounts will be disabled.
- Access to business applications will be revoked.
- Company data will be removed from approved BYOD devices where applicable.
- Employees must return any company-owned accessories or security tokens.

---

# Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Removal of BYOD privileges.
- Suspension of access to company systems.
- Disciplinary action.
- Security investigation.
- Legal action where applicable.

---

# Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed:

- Every 12 months.
- Following major cybersecurity incidents.
- After significant technology changes.
- When BYOD requirements change.

# Conclusion

Allowing employees to use personal devices can improve flexibility and productivity, but it also introduces cybersecurity risks. By following this BYOD Policy, employees and the organization can work together to protect sensitive information, reduce security threats, and maintain a secure working environment while supporting modern business operations.
