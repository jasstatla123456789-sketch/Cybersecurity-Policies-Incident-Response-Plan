# Business Continuity Plan

## Project

**Cybersecurity Policies & Incident Response Plan for a Small Business**

---

# Overview

A Business Continuity Plan (BCP) is a strategic document that ensures an organization can continue delivering its essential services during and after a disruption. These disruptions may result from cyberattacks, natural disasters, power failures, hardware failures, or human error.

The purpose of this plan is to minimize downtime, protect business operations, maintain customer trust, and ensure a rapid return to normal business activities.

---

# Objectives

The objectives of this Business Continuity Plan are to:

- Maintain critical business operations.
- Minimize service interruptions.
- Protect employees and customers.
- Reduce financial losses.
- Ensure availability of critical systems.
- Support disaster recovery activities.
- Maintain customer confidence.

---

This plan applies to:

- Business Operations

# Continuity Strategies

## Data Protection

- Daily backups
- Weekly full backups
- Encrypted backups
- Cloud storage
- Offline backup copies

---

## Communication

Alternative communication methods include:

- Company email
- Mobile phones
- Messaging applications
- Video conferencing
- Emergency contact list

---

## Remote Work

If the office becomes unavailable:

- Employees work remotely.
- VPN access is required.
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) must be enabled.
- Company-approved devices should be used.

---

## System Redundancy

Critical systems should include:

- Backup internet connection
- Cloud storage
- Backup power supply (UPS)
- Spare networking equipment

---

- File Server

Target Time: **2 Hours**

---

## Priority 2

Restore:

- Email Server
- Accounting Software
- Shared Drives

Target Time: **4 Hours**

---

## Priority 3

Restore:

- Company Website
- Employee Computers
- Printing Services

Target Time: **8–24 Hours**

---

# Emergency Response Procedures

## Cybersecurity Incident

- Notify IT Administrator.
- Activate Incident Response Plan.
- Isolate affected systems.
- Preserve evidence.
- Restore operations.

---

## Power Failure

- Switch to UPS.
- Save active work.
- Shut down non-essential systems.
- Contact electricity provider.

---

## Internet Failure

- Use backup internet connection.
- Notify ISP.
- Continue remote operations if available.

---

## Fire

- Evacuate employees.
- Contact emergency services.
- Protect life first.
- Recover systems after approval.

---

# Backup Strategy

| Backup Type | Frequency |
|-------------|-----------|
| Critical Data | Daily |
| Full Backup | Weekly |
| Archive Backup | Monthly |

Backup rules:

- Encrypt backup files.
- Store one copy offline.
- Test backups monthly.
- Keep multiple backup versions.

---

# Employee Responsibilities

Employees must:

- Follow cybersecurity policies.
- Report incidents immediately.
- Participate in continuity drills.
- Protect company equipment.
- Keep emergency contact information updated.

---

# Testing the Plan

The Business Continuity Plan should be tested through:

- Tabletop exercises
- Disaster simulations
- Backup restoration tests
- Remote work exercises
- Annual continuity review

Testing should occur at least once every year.

---

# Plan Maintenance

This plan should be reviewed:

- Every 12 months
- After major business disruptions
- Following cybersecurity incidents
- After significant infrastructure changes

---

# Success Criteria

The Business Continuity Plan is considered successful if:

- Critical systems are restored within target times.
- No critical business data is lost.
- Customers experience minimal service disruption.
- Employees continue essential operations.
- Communication remains effective throughout the incident.

---

# Conclusion

Business continuity planning enables small businesses to remain operational during unexpected disruptions. By identifying critical business functions, preparing recovery strategies, maintaining secure backups, and regularly testing continuity procedures, the organization can reduce downtime, protect customer confidence, and ensure long-term operational resilience.
